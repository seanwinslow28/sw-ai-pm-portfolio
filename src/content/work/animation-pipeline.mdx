<Opener>
  <p>I taught Seedance to draw me. Then I taught the pipeline to ship the drawing. The guy walking across the top of this page is the unit test. If a single frame breaks the line — if the jaw drifts, if the alpha channel leaks, if the encode quietly swaps in the wrong colorspace — the whole short breaks with it.</p>
  <p>The system is five agents and one shell script. The script cleans the frames. The agents make the harder calls: when the character has drifted enough to re-anchor, when an encode pass needs a re-run, when a 146-frame raw cycle is close enough to ship and when it isn't. I write the prompts. Seedance draws the model sheet. The pipeline does everything between sketch and render that I used to do by hand at New York Life, in a windowless basement, with three monitors and a copy of After Effects that crashed every fourth save.</p>
  <p>What's below is the production thread. What shipped, what was killed, what's still running. The 9.2-second loop at the top is rev 3 of the cleanup chain. Rev 1 took twenty-two seconds per frame and lost the alpha. Rev 2a takes four seconds per frame and holds the alpha through every encode. That's the whole job.</p>
</Opener>
