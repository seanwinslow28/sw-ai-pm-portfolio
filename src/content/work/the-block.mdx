<Opener>
  <p>Two years at a research firm whose customers ran money for a living and whose product was, depending on the day, a Snowflake share, a PDF, or a phone call returned within the hour. Campus was the surface the customers logged into — research, data, an API for the desks that wanted to wire it into their own models. RevOps was the other half of the building: the internal product that priced the tiers, tracked the renewals, kept the partner data legible to the analysts who didn't want to think about plumbing. Two products, one firm, the same calendar.</p>
  <p>The job was build it, ship it, then write it down carefully enough that the building kept running after I stopped being in it. 2024 was Campus v1 going out to a small set of institutional accounts through the partnerships team. 2025 was the tier structure locking and RevOps coming online inside the building. 2026 was the handoff doc, the runbooks, the institutional knowledge moved from people's heads into the system. The screenshots on this page have project keys redacted and customer names anonymized.</p>
  <p>The firm restructured around me; the products didn't. This page is about the products.</p>
</Opener>
