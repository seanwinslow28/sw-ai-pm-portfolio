## What is this?

A 5-test scoreboard that scores knowledge systems on the axes that matter when an agent has to act on the vault, not just read it: Persistent State, Defined Verbs, Ownership, Permissions, and a Queryable Audit History. Notion, Obsidian (default), Linear, and Sean's vault each get a row. Three passes, two failures — and the two failures are the architectural argument for what ships next.

## Why this approach?

[PLACEHOLDER — Q2] Most "PM tools" discourse is about how nice the editing surface feels. That's a content question. The agent question is structural: does the system have *typed edges* the agent can query without scanning, does the agent have *defined verbs* it can call without a free-form LLM bridge, does it have an *audit history* a reviewer can replay? Vault Scorecard is the diagnostic that turns the structural question into a single visible result. Comparison is the discipline — equality of presentation across all four systems is the honesty signal. The reader reaches the conclusion from the symbols, not from a `Sean's-vault-highlighted-row` accent.

## What would break?

[PLACEHOLDER — Q3] Three named failure modes: (1) the synthetic public vault fixture drifts from the production schema as `concept_edges` evolves — mitigation is the `scripts/generate_schema.py` build step that re-emits the ER diagram from the live SQLite file on every synthesis run; (2) the Notion/Obsidian/Linear rows go stale as those products ship features (Linear's recent RBAC additions push its OWN/PERM scores higher; the next Notion AI release may flip its STATE score) — mitigation is a quarterly rescore commit; (3) recruiters who arrive expecting a `Notion-killer` pitch bounce because the scoreboard is honest about Linear-better axes — that's the intended cost. The page filters for the audience that values calibration over salesmanship.

## What did I learn?

[PLACEHOLDER — Q4] Equality of presentation IS the credibility signal. The temptation was to highlight Sean's-vault row, or wash the passing cells in success green, or stack the columns in Sean's order. Resisting that produced a scoreboard a Linear PM could read without rolling their eyes. The two `HonestNote` callouts (Ownership + Permissions, both Linear-better) were the load-bearing part of getting that read — the mono register switch inside Newsreader essay prose signals "I am the author stepping outside the argument to calibrate it," which is what Tier-1 recruiters detect for.
